Soma Suzuki




I am currently a master's student in Center of Advanced Spatial Analysis in University College London. My main research interest lies in the agent-based human behavior modeling in the urban environment through inverse reinforcement learning. In particular, I am interested in generating human-like agents in urban simulation so that the acquired model is considered as an artificial laboratory that enables us to test ideas and hypothesis that are not easy to explore in the real world. My bachelor degree is in Physics from Kyoto University, where I worked on machine learning modeling of cohesive energy using random walk graph kernel. Before starting my master's course, I worked at MIT Digital Currency Initiative to address Open Music Initiative.
